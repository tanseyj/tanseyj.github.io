Andersonville

Location

Andersonville sits approximately 7 miles north of downtown Chicago, and a short walk to beautiful Lake Michigan. Many consider its unofficial boundries to be Bryn Mawr, Broadway, Foster, and Ashland; its heart is Clark Street.

A brief history

  • Early 1800s: Swedish farmland
  • 1850s: Cherry orchard north of Foster & east of Clark
  • 1870s: "Suburb" of Chicago where Swedish immigrants were allowed to build homes of wood -- outlawed in city limits after the 1871 Chicago Fire.
  • 1965: First Midsommarfest celebrated to honor Swedish roots
  • 1979: Women & Children First bookstore opens
  • 1980s: Many LGBT residents make Andersonville their home, spurring more development along Clark Street
  • 2015: 50th Midsommarfest celebrated to continue homage of local history
